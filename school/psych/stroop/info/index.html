What is the Stroop Effect?
The Stroop Effect is the delay between reaction time when different stimuli from something don't match (these stimuli are called incongruent). In this experiment, which is an example of a Stroop Test, the color of the text and the actual word spelling out a color name are the two stimuli. In the first set, the name and actual color match, so there is no incongruence (the stimuli are congruent). As a result, our brains have no trouble identifying the color of the font, because there is no contradictory information. Our top-down perception (the reading of the word) does not conflict with our bottom-up sensation (the registering of the color).
