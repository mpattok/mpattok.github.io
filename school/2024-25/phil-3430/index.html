Class Readings
Introduction: Poverty and philosophy
Aug 29

Poverty and contemporary political philosophy
  1. Jonathan Wolff, Edward Lamb and Eliana ZurSzpiro, “Poverty and Contemporary Political Philosophy,” in A Philosophical Review of Poverty (PDF)
  2. John Rawls, A Theory of Justice (selections on ideal theory) (PDF)
  3. Charles Mills, “‘Ideal Theory’ as Ideology” (PDF)
Poverty in the US and in the rest of the world
Sep 3

From the perspective of social science and policymakers
  1. John Iceland, “Characteristics of the Poverty Population,” in Poverty in America: A Handbook, 3rd edition (PDF)
  2. Office of Community Empowerment and Opportunity, Shared Prosperity 2017, pp. 8-11 (PDF)
  3. City Council of Philadelphia, Philadelphia Poverty Action Plan (2020), p. 1. (PDF)
  4. World Bank Group, Poverty and Shared Prosperity 2022, pp. 29-60 (PDF)

From the perspective of those living in poverty
  1. Linda Tirado, “Why I Make Terrible Decisions, or Poverty Thoughts” (PDF)
  2. John Scalzi, “Being Poor” and “‘Being Poor’ Ten Years On” (PDF)
  3. Deepa Narayan, et al., “The Definition of Poverty,” in Voices of the Poor: Can Anyone Hear Us? (PDF)
What is poverty? What is its normative significance?
Sep 10

“What is X?” Conceptual analysis
  1. Andrew Cullison, “Conceptual Analysis” (https://youtu.be/3B9l2EAeGEE)
  2. Scott Shapiro, Legality, Chapter 1, pp. 1-22. (PDF)
  3. Laura Valentini, Morality and Socially Constructed Norms, Introduction, pp. 15-6. (PDF)
Sep 12*

“What is X?” Conceptual ethics
  1. Alexis Burgess and David Plunkett, “Conceptual Ethics I” (PDF)
  2. Alexis Burgess and David Plunkett, “Conceptual Ethics II” (PDF)
Sep 17

Is poverty a direct or indirect phenomenon?
  1. Peter Higgins, Audra King, and April Shaw, “What is Poverty?” (PDF)
  2. Amartya Sen, “Poverty as Capability Deprivation,” in Development as Freedom, pp. 87-92 (PDF)
Sep 19*

Is poverty an ‘absolute’ or ‘relative’ phenomenon?
  1. S. M. Miller and Pamela Roby, “Poverty: Changing Social Stratification” in The Concept of Poverty, edited by Peter Townsend (PDF)
  2. Peter Townsend, “Poverty as Relative Deprivation: Resources and Style of Living,” in Poverty, Inequality, and Class Structure, edited by Dorothy Wedderburn (PDF)
  3. Amartya Sen, “Poor, Relatively Speaking” (skip section 8 if you want) (PDF)
