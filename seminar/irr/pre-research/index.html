Topic #1: Union Busting

Unions allow workers and employers to negotiate on more equal footing. However, many employers try their hardest to prevent workers from organizing into unions, thus creating an imbalance of power
Stakeholders: workers (specifically employees), business owners, politicians, union leaders/organizers

Title: Samsung boss sent to prison for union busting
Author: Al Neal		 https://peoplesworld.org/article/samsung-boss-sent-to-prison-for-union-busting/
  • South Korea's Seoul Central District Court sentenced Lee Sang-hoon, Samsung Electronics chairman of the board to 18 months in prison for union busting in violation of South Korea’s labor laws.
  • Several other management officials of Samsung were also sentenced
  • South Korea’s Trade Union and Labour Relations Adjustment Act of 1997 illegalizes
    • Preventing union formation
    • Conditioning employment on a lack of union membership
    • Conditioning employment on not forming a union
    • Firing workers based on joinig or planning to join a union
    • Firing workers for collective activities
